You have completed the end-to-end pipeline audit and successfully restored the trading flow. The system is now generating Signals → Opportunities → Strategy Decisions → Risk Approval → Orders → Positions. Before making any additional code changes, perform a second engineering audit focused on trading quality rather than simply making trades happen.

Your objective is NOT to increase the number of trades. Your objective is to maximize long-term paper trading quality and positive expectancy.

Perform a complete review of the following areas:

1. Signal Engine

* Verify whether the new thresholds are statistically justified.
* Determine whether the reduced thresholds introduce excessive market noise.
* Analyze whether thresholds should become adaptive instead of fixed.
* If no change is required, explain why.

2. Opportunity Engine

* Review the current scoring model.
* Evaluate whether spread, deviation and expiry alone are sufficient.
* Recommend additional scoring factors only if they materially improve trading quality.
* Avoid adding unnecessary complexity.

3. Strategy Engine

* Verify whether SCORE_OPEN, SCORE_WATCH and confidence thresholds are now properly calibrated.
* Confirm that the strategy is opening trades because of real opportunity quality, not because thresholds were artificially lowered.
* Recommend recalibration only if supported by data.

4. Position Sizing

* Review whether lowering score tiers weakens capital allocation logic.
* If possible, improve confidence calculation instead of lowering sizing requirements.
* Preserve risk discipline.

5. Mark-to-Market Pricing

* Verify whether unrealized PnL is calculated from Bid, Ask or Mid price.
* Determine whether the immediate stop-loss behavior is caused by spread accounting.
* If necessary, redesign valuation logic so spread is not treated as realized loss.

6. Exit Engine
    Audit every exit path:

* Stop Loss
* Take Profit
* Trailing Stop
* Time Exit
* Strategy Exit
* Risk Exit

Confirm each one functions correctly during paper trading.

7. Capital Management
    Review:

* consecutive loss logic
* cooldown mechanism
* daily loss limits
* exposure limits
* kill switch reset behavior

Ensure they behave realistically during long-running paper trading.

8. Performance Analytics
    Verify that the system can calculate and store:

* Win Rate
* Average Win
* Average Loss
* Expectancy
* Profit Factor
* Sharpe Ratio
* Maximum Drawdown
* Recovery Factor
* MAE
* MFE
* Average Holding Time
* Signal Precision
* Opportunity Conversion Rate

If any metric is missing, implement it only if it directly improves trading evaluation.

9. Paper Trading Realism
    Audit whether the simulator correctly models:

* spread
* fees
* slippage
* execution latency
* order fills
* partial fills (if supported)

10. Final Report

Do NOT immediately modify code.

First produce a detailed engineering report containing:

* Current pipeline status
* Remaining weaknesses
* Root causes
* Expected trading impact
* Recommended fixes ranked by expected improvement to profitability
* Risk assessment for every proposed modification

Only after the report is approved should implementation begin.

Maintain the existing architecture.

Do not introduce unnecessary abstraction, unnecessary refactoring, or over-engineering.

Prioritize real trading performance, robustness, and measurable edge over increasing trade frequency.
