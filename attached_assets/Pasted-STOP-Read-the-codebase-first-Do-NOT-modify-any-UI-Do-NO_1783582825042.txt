========================
ROOT CAUSE
========================

dynamic_weight is NOT a continuous realtime engine.

It is a scheduled batch worker that intentionally runs every:

DYNAMIC_WEIGHT_INTERVAL_SECONDS = 1800

and intentionally has

DYNAMIC_WEIGHT_RUN_ON_STARTUP = False

because there are no outcomes immediately after startup.

However main.py currently builds ONE list:

_enabled_engines

and uses the SAME list for TWO completely different purposes:

1. engine_health.register_engines(...)
2. run_watchdog_loop(...)

That means dynamic_weight is being monitored by the watchdog even though its natural cycle is 1800 seconds while watchdog expects activity within WATCHDOG_RESTART_SECONDS (600 seconds).

The watchdog therefore kills uvicorn after about 12 minutes.

This is an architectural mistake.

NOT a heartbeat problem.

========================
CORRECT FIX
========================

Keep dynamic_weight registered in engine_health so:

/api/v1/health/detailed

still reports it.

BUT

Do NOT include dynamic_weight in the list passed to:

run_watchdog_loop()

The watchdog should monitor ONLY continuous realtime engines.

dynamic_weight must remain visible in health reporting,
but must not participate in watchdog restart decisions.
